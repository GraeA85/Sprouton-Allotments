Our Allotment Story

Sproughton Allotments are based in the town of Sproughton, England. We are quite a small allotment site, however have quickly become well known in the town and nationally in the allotment community.

We started in 2002 when we were gifted a piece of land from the council, in the hopes of creating a community allotment space that everyone could benefit from. (We're the big green square on Church Lane!)

It took us over a year to get our site to what it is now, and we are always improving for the benefit of our allotment owners.

If you are interested in owning an allotment at Sproughton join our waiting list now and you could soon be reaping the benefits of allotment ownership!

The Allotment Site

Our Allotment site currently has 55 allotment plots, with plans submitted to add an extra 10 in 2023.

We designed our site around a circular gardened area to allow our allotment owners to gather and commune whenever they want.

The gate entrance is just off Church Lane - we encourage our allotment owners to walk where possible but have parking spaces available when you need to drop things off or pick things up. Our allotment shop is located in the centre of the allotment garden - see further down for more information.

We allow the keeping of chickens and other birds in our allotments, provided the area is kept clean and tidy to limit the attraction of pests.

Need more information about allotments?

The National Allotment Society have produced a video showcasing the joy and benefits of having an allotment. Everyone has to start somewhere and this video gives a good idea of the work involved and the benefits to your health and wellbeing of having your own little garden plot.

  • Mental wellbeing
  • Healthy activity - lots of exercise is involved!
  • Fresh and local seasonal fruit & vegetables - free of pesiticides
  • Massive sense of satisfaction
  • Contact with nature
  • A place to get away from it all
  • Helping the environment
  • Sense of community

Getting Started

You'll be surprised what little equipment you need to get your allotment started. You can grab a starter kit from our allotment shop or click below to discover what basics you need.

Equipment list

Essential Equipment List

  1. Spade
    Indispensible for your allotment. You'll be using it for turning the soil, digging out unwanted plants/weeds and preparing new beds. Invest in a strong one - you'll be using it every day!
  2. Fork
    Used for breaking up soil, lifting heavy roots and weeds. Also very handy at turning the earth to remove stubborn weeds such as bind weed.
  3. Watering Can
    Get one as big as you can carry, it means less trips around your allotment. Don't foget attachments - not all plants need a waterfall!
  4. Rake
    Mainly used for creating a level surface once you've dug over a bed. Also handy for collecting weeds and other debris in your plot.
  5. Hoe
    The best tool for weeding - don't waste time hand-weeding when you don't have to. Leave sufficient spacing between plants/crops to allow the use of a hoe, it will save you so much time!
  6. Wheelbarrow
    Moving soil/compost around your allotment can be a strenuous task - which makes having a wheelbarrow vital. Also handy for moving your produce to your car. Make sure to store it upside down to avoid rain collection and avoid wheelbarrows that have a punctureable wheel.
  7. Gardening gloves
    Gardening can be heavy and dirty work - protecting your hands as much as possible is vital. Don't spend too much on gardening gloves, you'll be replacing them often if you're anything like us!
